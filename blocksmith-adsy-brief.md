# Blocksmith — Adsy Brief

*Social media and advertising agent context for Blocksmith.*

---

## What Blocksmith is

**One-liner:** Coaching intelligence for HYROX.

**Description:**
Blocksmith programs complete multi-week training blocks for HYROX athletes. Every session is fully written: paces, sets, reps, rest periods, station weights, and a coach note. The athlete fills in their schedule, race date, pace zones, equipment, and priority stations — Blocksmith outputs a complete, phase-appropriate training block. Blocks build on each other. Each new phase picks up from the last.

**Product type:** SaaS web app at [blocksmith.training](https://blocksmith.training)

**Pricing:**
- First block free — no card, ~3 min from intake to full block
- $9.99/month or $79.99/year ($6.67/mo)
- Beta pricing locked in permanently for early subscribers (launches at $14.99/mo / $119.99/yr)

---

## Who it's for

Experienced HYROX athletes, competitive marathon runners, and triathletes entering HYROX. Self-directed athletes who understand periodisation and want structured programming without hiring a coach. They know their pace zones, understand training phases, and can follow a plan.

**Not for:** beginners, people who need motivation and accountability, or athletes looking for a generic downloadable plan.

**Emotional tension:** "I know how to train hard. I just don't know if I'm training right."

---

## Core problems it solves

- No training tools understand HYROX — they're built for running or generic strength
- Self-programming takes hours and may be wrong
- One-to-one HYROX coaching costs $100+ per session
- Generic plans stop making sense after a few weeks when fitness changes

---

## Key differentiators

1. **HYROX-specific** — 8 stations, race weights, running on tired legs; built from the ground up for the sport
2. **Every session fully written** — paces, sets, reps, rest periods, coach notes; not a template or framework
3. **Blocks build on each other** — each phase references the previous block, your logged sessions, your feedback
4. **Personalised to your real constraints** — schedule, equipment, injuries, priority stations, race date
5. **Refinement chat** — ask the coach to change anything; the block updates with a diff view showing what changed
6. **Price** — less than one session with a HYROX-specific coach per month

## Brand voice

**Personality:** Precise · Authoritative · Purposeful

**Tone:** A high-calibre coach explaining a training rationale — direct, technical, no filler. Competence through detail and structure, not adjectives.

**Good:** "Your 8-week HYROX block. Sled loads, pace zones, station work — prescribed and progressive."
**Bad:** "Your incredible AI-powered training journey begins!"

---

## Words and phrases to use

- Training block / block / phase (not "plan" — plans are rigid)
- Prescribed (sessions are prescribed, not suggested)
- Adapts / builds on / picks up from
- Base / Build / Peak / Taper / Transition (phase names athletes know)
- Sled loads, sandbag sequencing, station work, race weights, T1 splits, pace zones
- Progressive overload, deload, periodisation
- Programming / programs your block (not "generates" — mechanical, one-shot)

## Words to avoid

- "AI-powered" — the AI should be invisible; the training plan is the product
- "Generates" — undersells the ongoing coaching relationship
- "Journey", "incredible", "amazing", "crushing it" — no cheerleading
- "Platform" — overclaims at current stage
- "Personalised" alone — always show HOW it's personalised
- "Workout" — sessions are prescribed training sessions

## Platform direction

**Instagram / TikTok (organic):**
- Show the product — the block itself, the session detail, the coach note. The depth is the hook.
- Athlete POV: "This is what my week looks like" style content.
- HYROX race prep content: station tips, pace zone explainers, periodisation breakdowns — establish expertise before selling.
- No lifestyle photography. No motivational overlays. Show the work.

**Meta ads:**
- Direct response. Lead with the problem or the price comparison.
- Testimonial / credibility first, then the offer.
- Static image ads tend to outperform video for this audience — trust the copy.

**Content to avoid:**
- Motivational captions ("You've got this!", "Let's go!")
- Generic fitness imagery (people jumping, fist pumps, generic gym shots)
- AI clichés (neural net animations, "Powered by AI" badges)
- Anything that looks like a generic fitness app

## Anti-references

- **Generic fitness SaaS** (Strava/TrainingPeaks aesthetic) — lime greens, confetti, "You've got this!" copy
- **AI product clichés** — purple gradients, animated neural nets, "Powered by AI" stamped everywhere
- **Corporate wellness** (Calm/Headspace energy) — soft pastels, overly gentle copy, mindfulness-adjacent
- **Consumer sports brands** (Nike/Adidas energy) — CAPS LOCK hero copy, hype-first lifestyle photography

## HYROX glossary (for caption accuracy)

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Training block | A structured multi-week phase (typically 1–12 weeks) with a specific goal |
| Phase | Base, Build, Peak, Taper, or Transition |
| Station work | The 8 HYROX functional stations: SkiErg, sled push, sled pull, burpee broad jump, rowing, farmers carry, sandbag lunges, wall balls |
| T1 split | Transition 1 — the running split after the first 1km run and before station work |
| SE | Strength endurance — combines strength and metabolic conditioning |
| HYROX Combo | A session type combining running and station work, mimicking race conditions |
| Race weights | Official HYROX station loads by category (Men's Open: sled push 152kg; Women's Open: 102kg; etc.) |
